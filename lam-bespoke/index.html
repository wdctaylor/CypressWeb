Six Pillars for Social Success

Actionable strategies drawn from the brands above

Each of the benchmark brands above shares common principles that drive their success. Here’s how we’d adapt those principles into a tailored content strategy for LAM Bespoke.

01
Own Your Brand Identity
Refine @see_you_at_lam into a clear visual destination. A memorable profile photo, a bio that captures “the well-tailored home,” and a cohesive grid aesthetic that reflects LAM’s new traditional point of view—just like Tekla’s disciplined visual identity.
02
Invest in High-Quality Imagery
Showroom photography, styled vignettes, and custom fabric close-ups that match the editorial standard of @ameliatarbet and @shoppeamberinteriors. In the home décor space, visual quality is the price of entry.
03
Build Community, Not Just Followers
Engage with Houston’s design community—respond to comments, feature trade partners, run polls and Q&As in Stories. @thepottedboxwood grew to 243K largely by celebrating others’ work.
04
Use Hashtags with Intention
Targeted hashtags like #HoustonDesign, #BespokeInteriors, #NewTraditional, and #UpholsteredFurniture connect LAM to both consumers searching for inspiration and trade professionals sourcing vendors.
05
Collaborate Strategically
Partner with Houston-based interior designers, architects, and lifestyle photographers. Cross-tagging on project reveals expands reach organically—the same approach that fueled @abranca and @studiomcgee’s growth.
06
Post Consistently
Establish a regular cadence—3 to 4 feed posts per week, daily Stories—so the algorithm rewards the account and followers know when to expect new content. Consistency compounds over time.
